Blizzards: Understanding the Extreme Snowstorm

Blizzard Safety Tips

To stay safe during a blizzard, follow these tips:

  1. Stay Indoors: Avoid travel and stay indoors during a blizzard. If you must go outside, dress warmly and limit your exposure.
  2. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Keep supplies such as food, water, medications, and a battery-powered radio in case of power outages.
  3. Monitor Weather Reports: Stay updated with the latest weather forecasts and warnings to be aware of the blizzardâ€™s progress.
  4. Clear Snow Safely: Remove snow from driveways and walkways using appropriate equipment and avoid overexertion.
  5. Help Others: Check on neighbors, especially the elderly and vulnerable, to ensure they are safe and have adequate supplies.

What is a Blizzard?

A blizzard is a severe snowstorm characterized by strong winds, heavy snowfall, and reduced visibility. Blizzards can create extremely hazardous conditions, including whiteout conditions, where visibility is almost zero due to blowing snow. They can cause significant disruptions to travel and daily life.

Causes of Blizzards

  • Low Pressure Systems: Blizzards are often associated with low-pressure systems that bring cold air and strong winds.
  • Cold Air Masses: When cold air masses meet with moisture-laden air, it can result in heavy snowfall and blizzard conditions.
  • Frontal Boundaries: The interaction between cold and warm fronts can enhance snowfall and wind speeds, leading to blizzards.
  • Topography: Mountains and high terrain can exacerbate blizzard conditions by funneling and intensifying winds and snowfall.
  • Weather Systems: Large-scale weather systems like nor'easters can produce severe blizzard conditions over extended areas.

Types of Blizzards

Blizzards can vary based on their intensity and the weather conditions:

  • Severe Blizzard: Characterized by winds exceeding 35 mph (56 km/h), heavy snowfall, and visibility reduced to less than 0.25 miles (400 meters).
  • Moderate Blizzard: Winds between 25-35 mph (40-56 km/h), moderate snowfall, and visibility reduced to between 0.25-0.5 miles (400-800 meters).
  • Light Blizzard: Winds just below 25 mph (40 km/h), light snowfall, and visibility reduced to between 0.5-1 mile (800-1600 meters).
  • Snow Squall: A short-duration, intense snowfall accompanied by strong winds and reduced visibility, often leading to brief blizzard conditions.

Effects of Blizzards

Blizzards can have severe effects on people, property, and infrastructure:

  • Transportation Disruptions: Blizzards can close roads, halt public transportation, and create hazardous driving conditions.
  • Power Outages: Strong winds and heavy snow can damage power lines and infrastructure, leading to outages.
  • Property Damage: Accumulated snow and ice can damage roofs, trees, and other structures.
  • Health Risks: Exposure to extreme cold and strong winds can lead to frostbite, hypothermia, and other health issues.
  • Economic Impact: The cost of snow removal, property repairs, and lost productivity can be significant.
