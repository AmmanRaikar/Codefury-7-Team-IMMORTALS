Understanding Hurricanes

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a type of tropical cyclone that forms over tropical or subtropical waters. Hurricanes are characterized by low pressure centers, strong winds, and heavy rain. They can cause significant damage to coastal areas through wind, storm surge, and flooding.

Causes of Hurricanes

  • Warm Ocean Water: Hurricanes draw their energy from warm ocean waters, typically above 26.5°C (80°F).
  • Moist Air: High humidity in the lower to middle levels of the atmosphere fuels the storm.
  • Converging Winds: Winds coming together at the ocean's surface help to lift the warm, moist air, which powers the hurricane.
  • Low Wind Shear: Low variation in wind speed and direction with height allows the storm to grow without being torn apart.

Effects of Hurricanes

Hurricanes can cause a range of dangerous and destructive effects:

  • Strong Winds: Winds can exceed 150 mph, causing widespread damage to buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • Storm Surge: The rise in sea level associated with a hurricane can lead to severe coastal flooding.
  • Heavy Rainfall: Hurricanes can bring torrential rains, leading to flash flooding and river flooding.
  • Tornadoes: Hurricanes can also spawn tornadoes, adding to the potential for damage.

Hurricane Safety Tips

Preparation and timely action can save lives during a hurricane:

  1. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials like water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, and batteries.
  2. Plan Your Evacuation Route: Know where to go and how to get there if you are ordered to evacuate.
  3. Secure Your Home: Install storm shutters or board up windows, and secure loose outdoor items that could become projectiles.
  4. Stay Informed: Monitor weather reports and heed warnings from local authorities. If told to evacuate, do so immediately.
